## CIA = Confidentiality , Integrity, Availability

- this CIA triad is considered the foundational model for cybersecurity
- It encompasses the 3 characteristics of a system that must be maintained for it to be called secure.

  * Confidentiallity
  * Integrity
  * Availability

- Every tool, process, framework, decision etc . aims to protect one or more
- every breach woruld be a failure of one or more
- basic model but very integral
- Can use it as guiding north star for decision making .

### Confidentiality

	* Ability to allow authorized persons to access data or systems and prevents unauthorized persons from doing the same.

	* Encryption of data at rest and in transit play a large role.

	* Access levelsl for accounts and the permissions they are granted.

	* Failures in confidentiality can result to the leaking of personal and proprietory of data .

### Intergrity

	* Ensuring that data has not been tampered with and is actually from the entity who is supposed to be sending the data .
	* Ensuring data such as logs, ledgers, records are not modified without authorization
	* Checking data received from a sender matches what they sent via hashing or checksums
	* Use of certificates or other digital signatures to ensure a person or entity are who they claim or say they are.


### Availability

	* Systems , data and services are available to those who are authorized when they need them .
	* Keepings systems patched and updated .
	* Providing backups and failsafes to prevent downtime.
	* Preventing denial of services attacks such as DDOS .


### Conclusion

	* We can simplify all scurity related actions to maintaining one or more of the CIA triad .
	* Can ask simple questions like will this compromise or protect CIA
