# Blue Team = Cyber defenders

  * Blue team are the defensive side of cybersecurity
  * Responsible for protecting systems, networks and data.
  * In contrast to the red team

## Core Resoponsibilities

   * Monitoring systems for threats
   * Detect and respond to inncidents
   * Patch management and hardening
   * Deploy and design defenses
   * Perform risk assesments
   * Documentations and ticketing
   * Ensure compliance with regulations
   * And many more .


## SOC = Security Operation Center

   * Historically the SOC was a physical space (office/room/building etc.)

   * But now SOC is more than a place
   	 - Can still be refered to as a physical place
	 - Has become more centralized in location .
	 - Workers may be remote in different offices, or contracted out .
	 - Now the SOC usually refers to an entity
	 - Comprised of :
	   			 * people
				 * Tools/Tech
				 * Process


## Roles of a SOC
    * Monitoring
		- Watch for alerts from tolls and softwares
		- Look for unusual activity
	* Detection
		- Confirmation of events from the alerts
		- Perform proactive threat detectionn
		- Look for indicators of compromise(IOC)

	* Analysis
		- Perform investigations into events and incidents
		- Inspect and triage affected systems.
	* Responds
		- Come up with a remediation plan based on findings
		- Contain infected systems or threats
		- Document and report on findings

## SOC Analyst
   * Also refered to as a security analyst/engineer
   * Usually roles are tiered eg levels 1,2,3.
   * Level 1 analyst are mostly responsible for triage of alerts
   * More complex analysis, investigations and incidents response task escalated to higher tiers.
   * Frequently tasks are assigned and tracked through a ticketing system
   * Use various tools and software
   * Usually work will be guided by playbooks


## Incident Responder

   * Specialized in responding to inicidents and active threats.
   * Everything from breaches, ransomware , data theft ,malware and more .
   * will work to contain threats and limit the spread
   * sometimes will also perform digital forencics to gather evidence and perform further analysis.
   * Some organizations will hhave their own, but also is frequently contracted when needed.

## Security Engineers

   * This are the builders and maintainers of security infrastructure (hardware and software)
   * Works to harden the systems and improve defenses.
   * Frequently involved in processes such as patch management and updates.
   * Works with other roles in IT to ensure their deployments and projects are secure.

## Threat Hunter

   * Performs proactive searches for undetected threats that are already in the network systems
   * Study threat intel and analytics to be able to stay up to date.
   * Contribute to threat intel and other threat feeds .
   * Mostly used in larger organizations or mature SOCs.


## Digital forrensics

   * part of the incident response, but more concerned with evidence gathering and analysis
   * Responsible for proper handling of devices and evidences .
   * Creates copies artifacts of things like drives, logs, ram
   * Analyzes evidence to create a timeline and picture of the what, when and how of an incident
   * Supports legal, civil and compliance based investigations.


## Governanace Risk and Compliance
   * Responsible for ensuring that organizations comply with industry regulations and standards
   * Conduct risk assessments .
   * Audit security controls
   * Write policies and procedures .
